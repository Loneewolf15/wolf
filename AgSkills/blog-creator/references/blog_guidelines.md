# Blog Guidelines

These guidelines dictate how blog posts should be formatted and styled for platforms like Medium and Dev.to. 

## Structure

1. **Engaging Title:** Should be catchy, descriptive, and hint at the value the reader will get. (e.g., "Building a Custom Skill Agent in Python: A Step-by-Step Guide").
2. **Introduction:** Hook the reader. Briefly explain the project, what the specific update or feature is, and why it matters.
3. **The Problem/Goal:** What was the motivation behind this sprint or past two weeks of work?
4. **Implementation/Technical Deep Dive:** 
    - Explain *how* it was built. 
    - Include relevant, concise code snippets. Do not just paste huge blocks of code; focus on the core logic.
    - Mention architectures, patterns, or tools used.
5. **Challenges & Learnings:** Describe any roadblocks hit along the way and how they were resolved. This is highly valued on Dev.to.
6. **Conclusion & Next Steps:** Wrap up the post. State what is coming next in the project. Include a call to action (e.g., "Follow for more updates," or "Check out the repo").

## Tone & Style
- **Conversational yet Technical:** Write as if you are explaining a cool project to a fellow developer over coffee.
- **Formatting:** Use headings (`##`, `###`), bold text for emphasis, bullet points, and blockquotes for notes.
- **Platform Nuances:** 
    - **Dev.to:** Prefers highly practical, developer-centric content with lots of code examples.
    - **Medium:** Prefers a slightly more narrative-driven approach, focusing on the "why" and overarching architecture.
    - Aim for a balance that works well on both. Ensure Markdown is standard and clean.
