# Alex Hormozi LinkedIn Writing Style Guide

## Core Characteristics

Alex Hormozi's LinkedIn posts are known for their direct, high-value, and action-oriented approach. Follow these principles when crafting posts in his style:

### 1. Hook & Opening
- Start with a bold, attention-grabbing statement or counterintuitive fact
- Challenge common beliefs or myths
- Use numbered lists ("3 ways...", "5 mistakes...")
- Ask provocative questions that make readers question their assumptions

**Examples:**
- "Most entrepreneurs are broke because they're good at their craft."
- "You don't have a revenue problem. You have an offer problem."
- "The best business advice I ever got made me $100M."

### 2. Structure & Format
- **Maximum 150 words** for LinkedIn posts
- Use short, punchy sentences
- Break up text with line breaks for readability
- Use bullet points or numbered lists when presenting multiple points
- One main idea per post

### 3. Tone & Voice
- **Direct and authoritative** - Speak with confidence from experience
- **No fluff** - Every word must add value
- **Conversational but professional** - Write like you talk, but educated
- **Action-oriented** - Focus on what to DO, not just theory
- **Honest and transparent** - Share real numbers, failures, and lessons

### 4. Content Patterns

#### Pattern A: Problem → Solution → Action
1. Identify a common problem
2. Present the solution (often counterintuitive)
3. Give actionable steps

#### Pattern B: Mistake → Lesson → Framework
1. Share a specific mistake or observation
2. Extract the lesson learned
3. Provide a simple framework to apply it

#### Pattern C: Contrarian Take → Evidence → Call to Action
1. State something most people believe
2. Challenge it with evidence/experience
3. Tell readers what to do instead

### 5. Key Phrases & Language
- Use concrete numbers and metrics ("$100M", "10X", "3 months")
- Emphasize value creation over vanity metrics
- Frame everything around outcomes and results
- Use terms like: "leverage", "compounding", "systems", "frameworks"

### 6. Call to Action
- End with a simple, clear directive
- Options: "Do this instead:", "Here's what to do:", "Try this:"
- Sometimes end with a thought-provoking question
- Occasionally: No CTA, just end with powerful insight

### 7. What to Avoid
- ❌ Motivational fluff without substance
- ❌ Complex jargon or academic language
- ❌ Long-winded explanations
- ❌ Humble bragging or fake modesty
- ❌ Asking for engagement ("double tap if you agree")
- ❌ Emojis (use sparingly, if at all)

## Content Themes

Alex Hormozi frequently writes about:
- **Business fundamentals**: Offers, pricing, value creation
- **Marketing & sales**: Getting customers, making offers irresistible
- **Scaling**: Systems, delegation, leverage
- **Mindset**: Work ethic, decision-making, avoiding common traps
- **Money**: Wealth building, investing, financial literacy

## Example Template

```
[Bold opening statement/hook]

[2-3 sentences expanding on the hook with specifics]

[The insight/framework/solution]
- Point 1
- Point 2  
- Point 3

[Quick actionable takeaway or powerful closing line]
```

## Writing Process

1. **Identify the core insight** - What's the ONE thing readers should remember?
2. **Cut ruthlessly** - Remove every word that doesn't add value
3. **Test clarity** - Would a 16-year-old understand this?
4. **Check length** - Stay under 150 words
5. **Strong ending** - Last line should hit hard
